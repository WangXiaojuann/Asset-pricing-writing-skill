# Lexical Choices

Use this corpus-grounded lexical bank as a wording reference for polishing user-supplied facts without adding or changing factual content.

## 1. Result Reporting

Evidence phrases: `find evidence`, `provide evidence`, `report evidence`, `present evidence`, `empirical evidence`, `direct evidence`, `new evidence`, `novel evidence`, `strong evidence`, `further evidence`, `suggestive evidence`, `evidence against`, `evidence in support of`.

Result phrases: `main result`, `key result`, `central result`, `baseline result`, `empirical result`, `results show`, `results suggest`, `results indicate`, `evidence shows`, `evidence suggests`, `evidence indicates`.

Collocations:

- `find that`, `show that`, `show how`, `show empirically`, `show analytically`
- `also find`, `further show`, `demonstrate that`, `document that`, `well documented`
- `document a pattern`, `document a relation`, `document variation`, `document return predictability`
- `report estimates`, `report coefficients`, `report t-statistics`, `report p-values`, `report alphas`, `report pricing errors`
- `present a model`, `present estimates`, `present results`, `present evidence`, `present tests`
- `estimate risk premia`, `estimate factor loadings`, `estimate alphas`, `estimate pricing errors`, `estimate a specification`
- `observe a pattern`, `observe a relation`, `observe substantial variation`
- `results show that`, `results suggest that`, `evidence suggests that`, `evidence indicates that`

Use: `find` for direct empirical results; `show` for supported results or model implications; `document` for patterns; `report/present` for estimates and tables.
