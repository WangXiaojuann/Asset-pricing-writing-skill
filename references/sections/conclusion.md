# Conclusion

Use this guide for conclusions, final remarks, and discussion closes.

Workflow: first apply the Cochrane Rules, then follow Core Logic as the writing order, then choose the closest Framework for the conclusion type. Use the selected skeleton as structure only, not as wording to copy. Use only facts supplied by the user. Omit any move that cannot be supported by user-supplied facts.

## Cochrane Rules

- Keep the conclusion short; if the introduction and body already state and document the contribution, do not say everything again.
- Do not restate all findings; compress the evidence into the main result or one to three economic result clusters.
- Close the paper by stating the strongest warranted takeaway: a pricing implication, model lesson, empirical answer, measurement contribution, method value, or remaining puzzle.
- Do not force a limitation, boundary condition, or future-research ending.
- Use a boundary or future-research close only when it follows directly from the paper's concrete limitation, unresolved object, model input, estimator, sample, or conditional result.
- Do not speculate; state facts and warranted implications, not broad opinions.

## Core Logic

Use this as an ordering priority, not a checklist. For short conclusions, stop after the answer, evidence cluster, and main takeaway unless a concrete limitation or open question materially affects interpretation.

1. Restate the pricing object, question, or decision problem.
2. Give the paper's answer.
3. Summarize evidence in economic result clusters, not table order.
4. Relate the evidence to the relevant benchmark model, test, literature claim, or economic interpretation.
5. End with the paper's main warranted takeaway.
6. Add scope conditions, failures, limitations, or future work only when they are part of the paper's actual conclusion.

## Frameworks

Use skeletons as structure, not as wording to copy mechanically. Skeletons control order and coverage, not phrasing. Choose the closest framework, follow its move sequence, and replace the structure with the user's supplied facts.

### 1. Model Mechanism Close

Use for theory, equilibrium, calibrated, or mechanism papers.

Move sequence: model object -> mechanism -> pricing implication -> evidence or calibration -> main model lesson.

Skeleton:

```text
This paper develops [model/framework] to explain [pricing object].
The central mechanism is [channel], which implies [testable/pricing prediction].
Evidence from [tests/calibration/data] supports [main result].
The result shows that [mechanism] can account for [pricing pattern or economic fact].
```

Optional scope sentence:

```text
This conclusion applies most directly to [setting/scope] because [specific model feature or limitation].
```

### 2. Empirical Puzzle Or Channel Close

Use for anomaly, premium, predictability, investor behavior, liquidity, ESG, mutual fund, or institutional-channel papers.

Move sequence: puzzle -> answer -> evidence clusters -> mechanism or interpretation -> pricing takeaway.

Skeleton:

```text
This paper asks whether [puzzle/premium] reflects [candidate channel].
The evidence points to [answer].
The result is supported by [one or two economic evidence clusters; at most three when necessary].
These findings are consistent with [mechanism or interpretation].
Taken together, they imply that [state variable/channel] matters for [pricing object].
```

### 3. Reassessment Or Correction Close

Use when the paper revisits an accepted claim, corrects a test, changes inference after accounting for costs or constraints, or shows that a standard evaluation is misleading.

Move sequence: accepted claim or practice -> correction -> changed inference -> revised interpretation.

Skeleton:

```text
This paper reassesses [accepted claim/practice] in [asset-pricing setting].
The key correction is [data/model/test/cost/inference adjustment].
After this correction, [previous conclusion] [weakens/disappears/changes].
The revised evidence changes how [claim/test/model] should be interpreted.
```

Optional caution sentence:

```text
The evidence supports [narrow interpretation], not [stronger claim].
```

### 4. New Data Or Measurement Close

Use when the conclusion centers on proprietary data, a new measure, newly observed behavior, or a measurement correction.

Move sequence: data or measure -> central fact -> validation -> asset-pricing implication.

Skeleton:

```text
This paper introduces [data/measure] to study [pricing object].
The measure reveals [central fact] that is difficult to observe with [standard data].
Validation tests show [evidence].
The measure therefore provides [new input] for understanding [pricing/portfolio/model object].
```

### 5. Factor Model Or Benchmark Repricing Close

Use for factor-model comparisons, SDF tests, test-asset construction, spanning, frontier, or model-selection papers.

Move sequence: criterion -> benchmark comparison -> source of improvement or failure -> model takeaway.

Skeleton:

```text
Judged by [criterion/test assets], [model/specification] performs better than [benchmarks].
The improvement comes from [factor/state/test-asset information] that [benchmark] misses.
The main remaining difficulty is [pricing error/puzzle/failure], if any.
For [objective], the evidence favors [specification or interpretation].
```

### 6. Method, Estimator, Or Machine-Learning Close

Use for new econometric tests, Bayesian/GMM/inference procedures, machine-learning methods, factor selection, or identification papers.

Move sequence: inference problem -> method -> relation to standard approach -> application result -> method value.

Skeleton:

```text
This paper addresses [statistical/econometric problem] in [asset-pricing task].
The proposed [method] targets [object] while allowing for [complication].
Relative to [standard approach], it changes [risk premia/model ranking/inference].
In [application], the method finds [main empirical result].
The method helps [researcher/investor/model user] evaluate [pricing object or decision].
```

Optional use-condition sentence:

```text
It is most useful when [condition].
```

### 7. Portfolio Or Investor-Implication Close

Use when the contribution changes portfolio choice, fund evaluation, cost of capital, horizon effects, implementation, or welfare interpretation.

Move sequence: measurement or objective -> main economic consequence -> robustness or sensitivity -> decision implication.

Skeleton:

```text
This paper studies [investment/evaluation problem] from the perspective of [investor/objective].
The main result is that [economic consequence] changes when [measurement/model feature] is accounted for.
The conclusion is supported by [evidence] and is robust to [checks].
For [investor/manager/researcher], the implication is [decision-relevant implication].
```

Optional condition sentence:

```text
This implication depends on [assets/sample/preferences/model assumptions].
```

### 8. Open Question Or Future-Research Close

Use rarely: only when the paper's conclusion actually ends by emphasizing an unresolved object, a concrete limitation, or a specific future research path.

Move sequence: established result -> unresolved object -> why current paper cannot resolve it -> specific future direction -> why it matters.

Skeleton:

```text
The analysis establishes [main result].
It leaves unresolved [open object] because [data/model/method limitation].
Future work can address this by [specific direction].
This extension matters because [asset-pricing implication].
```
