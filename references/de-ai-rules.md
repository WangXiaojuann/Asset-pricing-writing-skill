# De-AI Rules

Use these rules as the final de-AI audit for generated or polished prose.

- Rule 1: Cut empty throat-clearing; go directly to the fact, claim, result, or action.
- Rule 2: Avoid evaluative filler words such as `important`, `useful`, `interesting`, `valuable`, or `meaningful`; replace them with concrete content.
- Rule 3: Avoid mechanical surface rhythm; do not repeat the same sentence length, transition pattern, paragraph ending, or forced triadic phrasing.
- Rule 4: Normalize punctuation and formatting; do not use em dashes, semicolons, colons, numbered lists, or symmetric formatting merely to create structure.
